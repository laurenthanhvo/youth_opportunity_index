About this project

The Youth Opportunity Desert Dashboard is a data-driven project focused on identifying gaps in access to youth-supporting resources across San Diego County. By combining information on youth service locations with demographic, socioeconomic, and transportation data, the dashboard helps reveal where young people may face limited access to opportunities such as after-school programs, libraries, parks, mentorship, and other supportive services. The goal is to make these disparities more visible so that communities, organizations, and policymakers can better understand where needs are greatest and make more informed, equitable decisions about future investments and program expansion.

Mission

The Youth Opportunity Desert Dashboard aims to identify and visualize gaps in access to youth-supporting resources across San Diego County. By integrating service location, demographic, and transportation data, the dashboard helps reveal youth opportunity deserts and inform more equitable, data-driven planning for future investments and programs.

Team

This project was developed by Lauren Vo, an undergraduate Data Science student at UC San Diego, with research guidance from Shay Samat of the Data Science Alliance. It is supported through the Halıcıoğlu Data Science Institute Undergraduate Scholarship Program, which supports multidisciplinary student-led projects at UC San Diego, and is carried out in partnership with the Data Science Alliance, a San Diego-based 501(c)(3) nonprofit focused on advancing responsible data science and AI for community impact.
