This dashboard maps the Youth Opportunity Index for San Diego County. You can compare places by census tract, ZIP code, or supervisor district, inspect overall opportunity or single domains, adjust domain weights, and compare the local YOI against the external Child Opportunity Index overlay.
The map uses a warm-to-cool score scale. Lower opportunity appears in red, orange, and light gold tones, while higher opportunity appears in light teal through dark teal tones. For the Youth Opportunity Index, scores are county-normalized for 2024. For the Child Opportunity Index overlay, scores are nationally normalized for 2023.
These two layers are not the same measure. The Youth Opportunity Index is this project’s local composite index for San Diego County and is county-normalized for 2024. The Child Opportunity Index overlay is an external benchmark layer and is nationally normalized for 2023. Because they use different inputs, normalization frames, and years, the same tract may look different across the two maps.
The weight sliders re-balance how much each domain contributes to the overall Youth Opportunity Index. When you are viewing Overall Youth Opportunity, changing the sliders immediately recomputes and recolors the map. When you are viewing a single domain layer, the sliders still affect the overall index and the profile diagnostics, but not that domain’s raw score.
Census tracts provide the most detailed neighborhood-level view. ZIP codes are broader and may be easier for general audiences to recognize. Supervisor districts are much larger policy geographies that are useful for county planning and stakeholder conversations. The map, selected feature, and profile/location details update to match whichever geography you choose.
The Domain Profile helps explain why a place scores the way it does. It shows domain scores, county-relative context such as percentile and rank, and highlights domains that may be acting as major opportunity gaps. You can click a domain entry to switch the map to that domain layer, and hover profile content for more detailed statistics.
A likely youth opportunity desert is a place with low overall opportunity and multiple weak domains, especially when youth supports, education, mobility, or health/environment are notably below the county pattern. The Domain Profile and Location Details panels are meant to help identify whether a place has broad deficits or only one or two concentrated weaknesses.
Overlays let you turn supporting map layers on and off. These include the YOI choropleth, tract boundaries, supervisor district view, transit routes, transit stops, service locations, and the Child Opportunity Index overlay. The COI overlay is meant as a reference layer, not as the same score system as YOI.
The Child Opportunity Index overlay is a reference layer you can use to compare your YOI patterns with an existing child-focused measure. It is most useful for checking where your local index agrees with or differs from the external benchmark.
Transit routes and stops help show whether lower-opportunity places may also face mobility barriers. Service locations highlight where youth-support and wraparound resources are currently located. Together, these overlays can help identify places where need appears high but reachable infrastructure or supports may be limited.
When hover is enabled, moving over map features and some overlays shows quick tooltips without needing to click. If you want a cleaner browsing experience, you can turn hover off and rely only on click selection.
“Copy current view link” copies a URL for the current dashboard state, including the selected geography when available and the active layer. “Copy selected tract GEOID” copies the currently selected tract identifier so you can paste it elsewhere for notes, documentation, or follow-up analysis.
Some geographies in the boundary layer do not perfectly match the population source used in the processed data. When that happens, the map can still display the feature, but some population-dependent statistics may appear as unavailable.
The copied link includes the selected tract and map layer when available.
